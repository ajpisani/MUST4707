Headings

Headings are used to structure your content hierarchically. There are six levels of headings in HTML:

H1: Main heading, highest level

The H1 tag is typically used for the main title of the page. It's the highest level heading and should be used for main headings only.

H2: Used for major section headings

H2 tags are used for major sections of your page. They serve as subheadings to the H1 tag and help organize content into manageable sections.

H3: Subsections under H2 headings

Use H3 tags for subsections within an H2 section. They help further organize content that falls under the same major section.

H4: Further subsections or points under H3

H4 tags are used for further dividing sections under H3 headings, ideal for topics that require a more detailed level of hierarchy.

H5: Detail under H4 headings

H5 tags are used for detailed points under an H4 section. They are less commonly used but are helpful for deep content structure.

H6: The lowest level heading

H6 tags are used for the lowest level headings. They can be used for minor headings under H5 or for footnotes.
